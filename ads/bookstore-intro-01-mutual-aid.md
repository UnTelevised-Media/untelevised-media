**What if buying a book was an act of mutual aid?**

We just launched Hurriya Publications — an independent bookstore built on a simple principle: the people who write the books should keep the money from the books.

Traditional publishers take 70–90% of every sale and call it "standard practice." We call it what it is: exploitation of creative labor. We built something different.

**How it works:**
- Authors keep the majority of every sale — more than any traditional publisher or marketplace, full stop
- Revenue splits are transparent, negotiated honestly, and public on every book's page
- We use sliding-scale terms based on socio-economic context — a writer surviving a genocide should not be negotiating from a position of desperation
- Readers can tip authors directly, outside the purchase flow, because solidarity shouldn't have to wait for a checkout button

This isn't charity. It's a structural correction. When you buy from Hurriya, you're not feeding a corporation that feeds a corporation that eventually, maybe, pays a writer. You are paying the writer. That's it. That's the whole chain.

We are not interested in books that fit the market. We are interested in books that have something true to say.
