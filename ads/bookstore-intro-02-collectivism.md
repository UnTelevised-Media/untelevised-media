Publishing is a feudal system.

A writer labors for years. A publisher acquires the rights for as little as possible. A distributor takes a cut. A retail platform takes another. By the time a sale happens, the person who made the thing gets a fraction of its value — if they get paid at all.

We are collectivizing that structure.

**Hurriya Publications** — the bookstore under UnTelevised Media — was built to invert the traditional model. Authors keep the majority. The economics are transparent. Revenue splits are negotiated based on the actual material conditions of the writer's life, not the leverage of whoever holds the contract.

Half our founding team has its roots in Gaza. This is not background information. It is the foundation.

We launched with one book. We are building this to hold many more — particularly from authors whose stories are underrepresented, whose economics are precarious, and whose voices the publishing industry has historically had no interest in amplifying because there was no safe profit in it.

**This is a collective publishing infrastructure. Come use it.**
