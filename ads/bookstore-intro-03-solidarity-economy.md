**We built a bookstore that works the way solidarity is supposed to work.**

Most of us have heard the critique of platform capitalism — Amazon, Spotify, the App Store — structures that position themselves as neutral intermediaries while quietly extracting value from every transaction, leaving the people who actually created things with scraps and a terms-of-service agreement.

Publishing is no different. The industry runs on gatekeeping: who gets a contract, who gets marketing, whose story is considered commercially viable. The writers who need the most support — those working from the margins of the economy, from conflict zones, from communities the literary establishment has never had to pay attention to — are exactly the ones the traditional model fails hardest.

**Hurriya Publications was built as an answer to that.**

We are an independent publisher and bookseller under UnTelevised Media. Here is how we actually operate:

**Authors keep the majority of every sale.** Not the industry standard 10–15% royalty. The majority. Revenue splits are listed publicly on every book page so readers can see exactly where their money goes.

**We use sliding-scale revenue terms.** If an author's material circumstances make the standard deal unjust — if they are surviving a war, if they are economically precarious, if standard publishing leverage would function as coercion — we adjust. We remove that leverage from the table entirely.

**Readers can tip authors directly.** Our platform includes a tipping system that lets readers send support directly to the writers whose work moved them. Outside the purchase flow. Because sometimes a book changes you and you want to do something about it beyond closing the tab.

**We run grassroots discovery campaigns.** A great book that no one can find is not a published book — it is a buried one. We do the work of getting it in front of people.

This is a solidarity economy in the specific sense: resources flow toward the people doing the labor, the structure is transparent, and participation strengthens the whole rather than extracting from it.

We are actively looking for more authors — especially those with stories the mainstream industry has no commercial interest in publishing. If that is you, or someone you know, reach out.
